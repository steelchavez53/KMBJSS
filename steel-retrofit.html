Structural Steel Retrofit

Structural steel retrofit is the process of adding, replacing, or modifying steel elements in existing buildings to improve their structural performance. This engineering intervention strengthens structures to withstand environmental forces (particularly earthquakes in California), correct design flaws, accommodate changes in building use, or address deterioration due to aging. Steel is preferred for retrofits because of its high strength-to-weight ratio, ductility, and reliability under seismic conditions.
